A.Vietnam's Economic situation

1.Overview

Vietnam labor market: Strong recovery

Despite the impacts from the complicated world economic and political situation, the Vietnamese labor market in 2022 still recorded a strong recovery. Supporting and promoting policies from the State play an important role in helping the labor-job market recover after the Covid-19 pandemic.

Firstly, the labor market has not kept pace with the requirements of socio-economic development, has not yet fully adapted to the economic restructuring and the process of industrialization, modernization, digital transformation and international integration.

Second, the institution of labor market development is still inadequate; there are no adequate mechanisms and policies on employment transactions, market quality management, regulations on labor standards and labor relations.

Thirdly, the labor supply-demand balance is not very effective and sustainable, leading to not approaching potential productivity (lack of local labor surplus, working in the wrong field of training, etc.). The market information system is not really complete. The connection between the domestic and international labor markets is still weak.

Fourth, the social safety net has low coverage, has not yet achieved high efficiency, and has only played the role of a support for part of the labor market.

Low notes of the labor market in the last months of 2022

In the fourth quarter of 2022, when the world economy and politics continued to develop complicatedly, causing the risk of global recession, Vietnam could not avoid negative impacts.

In that context, the underemployment rate of workers in the fourth quarter of 2022 was 1.98%, an increase of 0.06 percentage points compared to the previous quarter. Compared to the third quarter of 2022, the number of unemployed people of working age in the fourth quarter was more than 1.08 million people, an increase of 24.9 thousand people; unemployment rate in working age is 2.32%, up 0.04 percentage points.

Facing new challenges in 2023

Entering 2023, economic forecasts of all countries are somewhat pessimistic. Inflation is expected to weaken but has not yet fallen to the previous long-term average level as well as the market's expectation. Along with that, major central banks in the world such as the FED and the ECB continue to confirm that they will still pursue a tight monetary policy in 2023.

The biggest challenge to the market is that the ability of workers to change careers is still weak when the labor market information is still fragmentary and the skills and qualifications have not yet met the change of the employment structure.

Stay strong to overcome challenges

Although the recovery rate slowed down in the last months of the year due to the impact of the global crisis and the adjustments of the domestic market, it must be frankly stated that 2022 is still the year of recovery. impression of Vietnam's labor-employment market compared to 2021 and compared to the time before the epidemic in 2019. This will still be a good step to help the market continue to recover and develop in 2023.

If relevant authorities are determined to accompany workers and employers to overcome the challenges of 2023, it is certain that the labor market will continue to have a strong year of recovery and development. make a significant contribution to socio-economic recovery and development not only in 2023 but also for the next period.

2. How is the pay:

According to the latest statistics (2022), the median monthly salary in Vietnam is 6.5 million VND per month (source) or approximately $275 USD/month. Salary range: 4,360,000 VND/month (minimum salary) to the highest average of 76,900,000 VND/month (actual monthly salary may be higher).

  • 1.Marketing: Marketing is one of the fastest growing industries in Vietnam, so the salary for this industry's personnel always ranges from 8 to 20 million VND/month or maybe more depending on capacity.
  • 2.Human resource: Human resources is an indispensable part in any company, it plays an important role in the success of the company. Therefore, this is also an industry with very good salaries for employees. About 8 million VND for fresh graduates, 15-20 million VND for department heads, some places even pay 30-80 million VND for highly qualified employees.
  • 3. Finance: Although the salary for new graduates in this major is about 5 to 6 million VND/month, about 25% of management level employees enjoy a salary of up to 70 million VND.
  • 4. Tourism: This industry is always on the list of easy jobs because the development demand in Vietnam is very high. Employees in this industry often have a high starting salary of 6 to 8 million VND/month (excluding bonuses and commissions), as well as good incentives and benefits.

B. About Recruitment:

1. Languages and cultures

Vietnamese is the official language in Vietnam. While many professionals can communicate in English, it can be advantageous to have basic knowledge of Vietnamese or hire bilingual staff. Understanding Vietnamese culture, customs, and business etiquette will also help you establish good working relationships.

2. The labor market

  • Labor Laws

    Familiarize yourself with Vietnam's labor laws and regulations. The Labor Code of Vietnam governs the employer-employee relationship and covers aspects such as working hours, wages, leave entitlements, termination procedures, and more. Understanding these laws will ensure compliance and help you create fair and legal employment contracts.

  • Work Permits

    Foreigners working in Vietnam generally require a work permit. Ensure that you and your employees have the necessary permits before commencing employment. Work permits are typically obtained through the Vietnamese employer sponsoring the foreign employee.

  • Employment Contracts

    Draft clear and comprehensive employment contracts that comply with Vietnamese labor laws. Contracts should include details such as job description, working hours, salary, benefits, probationary periods, and termination clauses.

  • Compensation and benefits

    Research the prevailing market rates for salaries and benefits in your industry in Vietnam. Ensure that the compensation you offer is competitive to attract and retain talent. Common benefits include annual leave, public holidays, sick leave, health insurance, and social insurance contributions.

  • Taxes and Social Security

    Familiarize yourself with the tax obligations and social security contributions required for both the employer and the employee. Ensure that you comply with all tax and social security regulations, such as making monthly contributions to the social insurance fund.
